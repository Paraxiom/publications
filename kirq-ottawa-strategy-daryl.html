Why This Is Compelling

1. The Gap Is Real

Ontario has zero QKD testbeds. The only Canadian testbed is Kirq in Quebec (Numana — Sherbrooke, Montreal, Quebec City). The nation's capital, where CSE, DRDC, and federal procurement decisions are made, has no quantum network infrastructure. This is genuinely embarrassing given Canada's quantum ambitions and $334M commitment.

2. The Demand Is Guaranteed

DRDC's Quantum 2030 plan explicitly calls for "a communications network capable of transmitting quantum information over long ranges." CSE needs to evaluate PQC solutions. Federal departments have CNSA 2.0 mandates. These aren't hypothetical customers — they're obligated buyers who happen to be walking distance from any Ottawa colocation facility.

3. The Cost Is Reasonable

Tier 1 seed node: $130–230K. The software already exists (Kirq Hub is open source). We're buying hardware and racking it, not building from scratch. Compare: China spent billions, EU is spending €90M+, even Quebec's Kirq is $10M+. Our seed is 1% of that.

4. Grant Ecosystem Is Rich

NRC Quantum Internetworking launching 2026. FedDev Ontario already funds quantum companies in the region ($17.2M across 12 companies). NSERC Alliance wants university-industry partnerships. A $180K seed investment could attract $500K+ in matching grants within 12 months. The 3–4x leverage is realistic.

5. Crypto4A Is Right There

They manufacture quantum HSMs at 1550 Laperriere Avenue, Ottawa. They received $3.75M from FedDev Ontario. Making their QxHSM a launch partner for Kirq Ottawa is natural — it's a showroom for both of us. Their QRNG becomes our first entropy source, our network becomes their demo environment.

6. Timing Is Perfect

2026 is the "Year of Quantum Safety" (#YQS2026). QEYSSat satellite launches late 2026. NRC Quantum Internetworking starts this year. Budget 2025 allocated $334.3M for quantum in defence. The window is open now — if we don't move, someone else (possibly Numana) will expand into Ontario.

Risks and Mitigations

Honest assessment. What could go wrong and how we handle it.

Risk 1: Numana Territorial Conflict

Their Kirq testbed in Quebec shares the "Kirq" name (coincidence — ours is from our own codebase). They might view Ottawa expansion as us stepping on their territory.

Mitigation: Approach Numana early. Frame Ottawa as a national Kirq expansion node, not a competitor. Offer to integrate with their existing network. Make them a partner, not an adversary. Ideally, get a letter of support for grant applications.

Risk 2: No Local Ottawa Presence

We're Montreal-based. Grassroots credibility in Ottawa requires someone physically there — attending meetups, visiting NRC, meeting Crypto4A, sitting in university labs.

Mitigation: Three options: (1) Daryl relocates/commutes if Ontario-based, (2) hire a part-time Ottawa-based contractor or Carleton grad student, (3) MITACS intern from uOttawa/Carleton. Cost: $0–50K depending on approach.

Risk 3: Overcommitting on Timeline

Tier 1 is doable in 6 months. Tier 3 (cross-provincial link) is multi-year, multi-million, requires telco partnerships (TELUS, Bell). Easy to promise too much too early.

Mitigation: Only commit to Tier 1 initially. Each tier is independently valuable. Don't promise Tier 3 to get Tier 1 funding. Let results drive expansion.

Risk 4: Hardware Vendor Dependency

If Crypto4A's QxHSM pricing is too high or delivery is slow, Tier 1 stalls. We need at least 2 independent QRNG sources for meaningful aggregation.

Mitigation: Start conversations with multiple vendors simultaneously: Crypto4A, IDQuantique, Quantum eMotion (Montreal). IDQ Quantis PCIe cards are $3–5K — these can be purchased immediately as a baseline source while negotiating Crypto4A terms.

Risk 5: Funding Delays

Government grants take 3–12 months to process. If we wait for funding before starting, we miss the 2026 window.

Mitigation: Bootstrap Tier 1 with internal capital ($130–230K). Use the running node as proof-of-concept for grant applications. A working demo is 10x more fundable than a proposal.

Risk 6: Revenue Is Distant

A quantum entropy node doesn't generate direct revenue in Year 1. This is infrastructure, not a product.

Mitigation: Revenue comes from: (1) consulting/integration services for government adopters, (2) grant funding covers operations, (3) the node makes our other products (qssh, qssl, Coherence Shield) more credible and sellable. It's a sales accelerator, not a profit center.
